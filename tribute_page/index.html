Steve Jobs

The American entrepreneur and cofounder of Apple, who pionered some of the most innovative technologies in history, including the famous iPhone.

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs' life:

  • 1955 - Steven Paul was born in San Francisco
  • 1969 - Steve Jobs meets Steve Wozniak, 5 years his elder, through a mutual friend. Woz and Steve share a love of electronics, Bob Dylan, and pranks.
  • 1974 - Steve gets his first job at video game maker Atari, and later makes a trip to India to 'seek enlightenment' with his college friend Dan Kottke.
  • 1976 - Woz and Steve show off the early Apple I board at the Homebrew Computer Club.
  • 1976 - Apple Computer Inc. is incorporated by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ron Wayne.
  • 1977 - Apple makes a huge sensation at the West Coast Computer Faire with a prototype Apple II.
  • 1978 - The Apple II becomes the first mass-market personal computer, with impressive sales around the US. Apple becomes a symbol of the personal computing revolution.
  • 1979 - Steve Jobs is shown the first working graphical user interface at Xerox PARC.
  • 1980 - Apple goes public, increasing Steve Jobs' net worth from dozens of millions of dollars to over $200 million.
  • 1985 - Steve Jobs resigns from Apple and starts NeXT with five other refugees from Apple. Apple announces it will sue NeXT.
  • 1996 - Apple, which was desperately looking for a modern operating system to buy, eventually buys NeXT for $400 million. Steve Jobs is named "informal adviser" to Apple CEO Gil Amelio.
  • 2000 - Steve Jobs drops the 'interim' in his title and officially becomes Apple's CEO. He also demoes Mac OS X's revolutionary Aqua interface to a bewildered audience.
  • 2003 - Steve Jobs is diagnosed with pancreatic cancer, but stubbornly refuses any modern medical treatment for months. He tries alternative diets instead.
  • 2006 - The Walt Disney Company acquires Pixar for $7.4 billion. Pixar's largest shareholder Steve Jobs joins the Disney board while Ed Catmull becomes president of the Walt Disney Animation Studios, and John Lasseter its chief creative officer.
  • 2007 - In his most memorable keynote presentation ever, at Macworld 2007, Steve Jobs introduces iPhone and its revolutionary touch-screen interface. He also introduces Apple TV and announces the company's name change from Apple Computer Inc. to Apple Inc. to better reflect its new nature.
  • 2008 - At Macworld 2008, Steve Jobs introduces MacBook Air, with the tagline 'the world's thinnest notebook'. Three years later, it will come to redefine all of Apple's notebook product line.
  • 2009 - Steve receives a liver transplant at the Methodist University Hospital in Memphis, Tennessee. He was weeks away from dying when he got the surgery.
  • 2011 - Jobs surprises the world by announcing his new medical leave of absence, without any end date.
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs resigns as CEO of Apple. Tim Cook becomes Apple CEO.
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs dies at home, surrounded by his family.
